HOW TO COMPLY
Documentation & Roles
Energy Compliance Pathways
Documentation Requirements by Path
Tiered Prescriptive
Before Construction

Determine how you will achieve 10 points before breaking ground.

Required Documentation
  • Wall detail drawings (at building permit)
  • Building plans showing volume (at building permit)
  • Window schedule with specs (at framing)
  • Equipment submittals for HRV/water heater (at final)
  • EnerGuide report if claiming airtightness (at final)

No third-party reports required unless claiming airtightness points.

Tiered Performance
Pre-Construction
  • Engage an Energy Advisor to model your design
  • Review model results to confirm compliance
  • Submit EnerGuide pre-construction report to AHJ
At Project Completion
  • Blower door test (site assessment)
  • Final EnerGuide Full House Report
  • Performance Path Completion Form

Energy Advisor involvement is required throughout.

Building Official Role

Energy compliance fits within your existing inspection process. The key is knowing which path the builder is using.

For Prescriptive
  • Collect documentation at each inspection stage
  • Verify installed upgrades match documented point selections
  • No third-party reports to review (unless airtightness claimed)
For Performance
  • Receive the Performance Path Completion Form at project end
  • You do not review the energy model itself
  • Confirm form is complete and signed by certified EA
Energy Advisor Role Performance Path Only

Energy Advisors are energy efficiency and modeling experts. They:

  • Create HOT2000 models to demonstrate compliance
  • Help identify changes if initial design doesn't pass
  • Conduct blower door tests and produce EnerGuide reports
  • Complete the Performance Path Completion Form

Engage your Energy Advisor early — ideally at design stage. Early modeling catches issues before they become expensive mid-build changes.
